# Final Design Direction — Professional Software Agency

The website is moving in the right direction.

However, I want the visual style to feel more like a professional software company and less like a designer portfolio.

Do not redesign the website again.

Instead, refine it using senior-level UI/UX principles.

---

## Design Goal

When a visitor opens WebAksh, they should immediately think:

"These people build serious software products for real businesses."

Not:

"This looks like a creative design experiment."

---

## Reference Quality

Use inspiration from:

* Stripe
* Vercel
* Linear
* Notion
* Framer
* Raycast

Focus on:

* Professional presentation
* Product-first storytelling
* Strong hierarchy
* Trust and credibility

Avoid overly artistic portfolio styles.

---

## Typography Rules

Current typography still feels slightly oversized.

Reduce large headlines where necessary.

Prioritize:

* Readability
* Hierarchy
* Balance

Use:

* Large headlines only where they add impact
* Medium headlines for most sections
* Comfortable line lengths
* Better paragraph spacing

The content should feel easy to consume.

---

## Product-First Design

Show actual work.

Use:

* Dashboard previews
* Admin panels
* Mobile app screens
* Website screenshots
* Product mockups

A visitor should understand within 5 seconds what WebAksh builds.

---

## More Professional Layouts

Reduce excessive empty space.

Balance:

* Headline
* Supporting text
* Visuals
* CTA

Every section should communicate value.

Avoid sections that are mostly typography.

---

## Refine the Hero

Hero should contain:

### Clear Value Proposition

What WebAksh does.

### Supporting Text

Who we help.

### CTA

Start a Project

View Case Studies

### Product Preview

Real project showcase.

The hero should feel like a software company homepage.

---

## Trust & Credibility

Add stronger trust signals:

* Technologies used
* Industries served
* Development process
* Support process
* Project outcomes

Avoid fake statistics.

Use real credibility.

---

## Services

Focus on business outcomes.

Instead of:

"We build websites"

Use:

"We create high-performance websites that help businesses attract customers and generate leads."

Every service should explain:

* Who it helps
* What problem it solves
* Expected outcome

---

## Case Studies

Case studies should feel premium.

Include:

* Client challenge
* Research
* Design process
* Technology stack
* Screenshots
* Results

Use large visuals.

Show real work.

---

## Motion Design

Keep animations subtle.

Use:

* Smooth fade-ins
* Text reveals
* Hover states
* Micro interactions

Avoid flashy animations.

Avoid animations that exist only for decoration.

Every animation should support usability.

---

## Visual Polish

Improve:

* Consistent spacing
* Consistent margins
* Card alignment
* Visual rhythm
* Section balance

Everything should feel handcrafted.

---

## Final Objective

Build the website as if it were created by:

* A senior UI/UX designer
* A senior frontend engineer
* A professional software agency

The website should feel premium, trustworthy, modern, and conversion-focused.

Quality over effects.

Clarity over decoration.

Professionalism over experimentation.
